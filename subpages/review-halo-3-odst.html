Halo 3: ODST

Below is my written Halo ODST review. My campaign series: Halo 3: ODST [Arbaaz]

Published • 18 September 2022

Halo 3 ODST is a first person sci-fi slow-paced story-based shooter...

And you see the battlefield as each member of the squad. In New Mombasa, a city on Earth, a team of Orbital Drop Shock Troopers are deployed to repel the invading Covenant forces (Buck, Dutch, Romeo, Mickey, Rookie). Before they land, a slipspace jump from a nearby Covenant ship falters their course and they end up scattered from their drop point. Alone, they need to regroup and take out as many Covenant as possible. Fight across city streets, zoos, and military bases in this adventure, use human and Covenant weapons to your advantage. The city is aware, due to a superintendent-class AI that manages traffic systems and other infrastructure, maybe it can be help you on your way.

Gameplay

The gameplay is neat and energetic, roaming through the levels and using unique and familiar weapons to batter the Covenant with is always fun, just this time it's performed by elite soldiers than genetically modified, heavily armoured Spartans. The chapters are lengthy and there are some story cutscenes to break up the action or travelling. There is a map and objective menu to guide you through the levels. There aren't any armour abilities to use, but you can still pick up a plasma cannon or minigun for extra firepower. There are vehicle sections which add some excitement to the flow, but I found that the game felt more humble than spectacular. Just like Reach though, I think it is an interesting and thoughtful take on the Halo game series compared to the main ones.

Audio Visual

There are varied environments like a zoo, ONI base, subway system, and rooftops to explore. The colours can be generally washed out and grey, but flashing lights, scattered vehicle debris, and cool building structures can set the scene for New Mombasa. The gunplay feels good, but the HUD is more sparse without a radar and active ability shown. The sound effects are nice and the music has a remixed feel, even some jazz additions. It's also cool to sometimes hear character comments during a skirmish. Sometimes you can find extra lore from terminals about a side story within the city. Occasionally I like to flick night vision on and off to scan for enemy outlines, which is one of my gamer habits if I am presented with it.

Pros

  • Interesting campaign
  • Firefight mode (haven't tried)
  • Nice music and ambience effects

Cons

  • Some sections are dull
  • No abilities
  • Not amazing graphics

Conclusion

Halo 3: ODST is a creative sci-fi shooter which balances raging battle fronts against the sombre night streets. I score it 7/10.

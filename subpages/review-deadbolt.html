DEADBOLT

Below is my written Deadbolt review. My playlist: DEADBOLT [Arbaaz]

Published • 25 September 2022

Deadbolt is a 2D side-scrolling stealth-based pixel art shooter...

And it silently guides you to become an efficient assassin. In this game you play as a Reaper, employed by the Flames to cull the undead. They live miserably in a state of limbo and also cause trouble in the world, requiring their dispatchment. Zombies, vampires, skeletons, and demons wander the Place, and even form groups led by undead gang leaders. Observe the enemy movements within rooms, flick off the lights to hide, turn to smoke and zip through vent shafts. Explore possible routes and techniques to complete the challenging levels, to get the best score and time. Be careful above all else, for the Reaper dies in one, fleeting shot.

Gameplay

The gameplay is tactical and offers moments of celerity. Enemies are quick to react, so you must find how and where to hit them first. The darkness can be used to conceal you, so lights can be shot. Doors can be knocked on to distract nearby enemies, and you can evade them by using vents in the buildings. There's some creativity in the way each level can be completed, but not plenteous. Enemy walk and guard routines are fairly robotic, and while there is some randomness for the distance they travel, the game feels based on timing and not skill or techniques. Levels are short to play and the game is only a couple of hours long at most. It offers a level editor which is a fun feature. There are a handful of guns to acquire but my gripe is that you can only use two for the mission and the rest are locked, even though you own them.

Audio Visual

The graphics are quite simplistic, and there is some detail to levels, but nothing impressive. I like how there is a story crafted for this game, with snippets of lore and background information about the factions. Something that I find interesting is the dynamic gore system, where blood splatters on the walls are splodgy and creates a distinct gruesome style for this game. The soundtrack is nice which blends rock with jazz themes and even disco or metal tones depending on the song. Light is an important presence to consider in the way each level is played - whether you want to lure enemies somewhere or to sneak up on them. I think some of the particle effects like fire and dust sparkles are a bit mesmerising. A nice touch is how your room gets improved over the course of the game, as you become a more successful Reaper.

Pros

  • Level editor
  • Good soundtrack
  • Detailed factions and story

Cons

  • Short game
  • Shallow game mechanics
  • Weapon restrictions

Conclusion

Deadbolt is a challenging stealth-based shooter which is nice to play in the quiet hours. I score it 7/10.

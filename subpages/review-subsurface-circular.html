Subsurface Circular

Below is my written Subsurface Circular review. My video: Subsurface Circular - Gameplay

Published • 26 July 2022

Subsurface Circular is a third person robotic singleplayer text-based adventure...

And it's completely underground. In this game you play as a Tek, more specifically a detective that travels to different subway stations on the underground network to gather information from passengers. A new snippet of information has risen, describing the disappearances of Teks in the city above. Talk to strangers and figure out what they know, learn about the Tek world and the purpose of these robots to gain insight into the problem. Unlock new focus points and solve puzzles for more dialogue.

Gameplay

The gameplay is simple and easy-going in Subsurface Circular, the point and click nature and customisable dialogue speed reduces the amount of inputs and distractions the player can sense so they can focus on the story events more. You get to meet a variety of different Teks like librarians and fabricators who all have a background and a story to tell about themselves. Except listeners, because they are busy with their music and won't even respond to you! The game is quite relaxing and short, where you can complete it in one sitting, but the pace is quite slow and toned down which may not satisfy everyone. There are robots everywhere, so if you have automatonophobia it will not be pleasant. I like robots so here I am writing this review.

Audio Visual

The animation style, soft colour palette, and blur effects create a unique and cosy atmosphere in the game world. The train doesn't change but it's exciting to see the new faces at each station or chapter. A boppy electronic soundtrack is a nice accompaniment to the flow of the game. The characters don't have voices but communicate to each other via a text channel. I think that's a clever concept but on-screen it means a lot of reading for the player. The robot designs are detailed and look cool with their LEDs. The UI is simple but arranged with respect to the camera to let the text pop and show the speaking Teks.

Pros

  • Interesting story
  • Cool game world
  • Simple gameplay

Cons

  • No real replay value
  • Short game
  • Needs changes of scene

Conclusion

Subsurface Circular is a steady and thoughtful detective game. I score it 7/10.


Information

  • Release date: 2017
  • Developer: Mike Bithell
  • Platforms: PC, Nintendo Switch
